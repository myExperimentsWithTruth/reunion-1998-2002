Some nights we still feel it.
The late-night itch, even now, even after we have quit our vices.
The small pieces at Shama, split six ways because someone was always broke. The cycle ride to Lalkuan without a ticket, the choti line, and Mr Fareed’s free rides to Lucknow on the NT.
That mad cycle ride out to Bishtji, and then all the way back.
The sweltering humidity, the powercuts, choti badi, and what not.

Much of it is gone now. The shops have changed hands, the cycles are gone, and gone too are the boys and girls we were. We have careers and grey hair and children taller than us.

We landed there by chance.
We are bonded now, forever.

But tell me honestly.
Would you not give anything to live one of those days again?

Twenty-five years is long enough to forget. It is also just long enough to come back. Put your name down, and let us find every last one of us.

The road out to Bishtji is still there. So are we.
